KakoBuy QC Checklist — The Complete Quality Control Guide (2026)

Published: April 20, 2026Updated: May 26, 2026

Why a QC Checklist Is Non-Negotiable

QC KakoBuy is not a suggestion — it is the most important habit you can build. Warehouse QC photos are your only window into what you are actually getting before you pay for international shipping. A structured QC checklist ensures you never skip a critical check because you were in a hurry.

The Universal QC Checklist

Every item, regardless of category, should pass these checks before you mark it as approved: compare against listing photos side by side, verify size tag matches what you ordered, check stitching consistency, confirm logo and print placement, and inspect material quality in close-ups. Our shoes guide and hoodies guide have category-specific additions.

Category-Specific QC Criteria

Shoes need insole measurements and sole alignment checks. Hoodies need GSM verification and embroidery density inspection. Jerseys need text verification on every stitched element. Each category has its own QC lens — use the right one.

QC Checklist by Category

CategoryKey QC ChecksMust-Request Photos
ShoesInsole length, sole alignment, toe boxInsole+ ruler, top-down, heel, logo
HoodiesGSM, embroidery, size chartChest+ ruler, logo macro, tag
T-ShirtsPrint quality, blank weightPrint close-up, chest+ ruler
JacketsFill density, zippers, stitchingFlat lay, hardware close-up, tag
JerseysText accuracy, stitch densityName/number close-up, badge macro
Red Light Triggers: Misspelled names, crooked logos, wrong size tag, visible glue stains, missing hardware. If you see any of these, mark RL and move on.

Frequently Asked Questions

Insole measurement with ruler (shoes), chest/length with ruler (clothing), logo close-ups, size tag, material texture close-up, and any custom elements like embroidery or prints.

Mark the item as RL in your spreadsheet, document the specific issue, and request a return to the seller. Do not ship items you are unhappy with — return shipping internationally is rarely worth it.
