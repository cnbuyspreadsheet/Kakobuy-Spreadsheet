KakoBuy Jackets: Puffer QC, Fill Power & Winter Guide (2026)

Published: April 20, 2026Updated: May 26, 2026

Jackets: The Expensive Category

KakoBuy jackets — especially puffers — are the heaviest and bulkiest items in any haul. A winter parka can weigh 1.5-2.5kg and consume significant volumetric space. Budget carefully: a 2kg jacket at ¥150/kg costs ¥300 just to ship.

Puffer QC: Fill Assessment

Request photos of the jacket laid flat from multiple angles. Assess loft (puffiness) — a flat jacket may need 24-48 hours to expand after vacuum packaging, but severely underfilled jackets will not recover. Check fill distribution: no empty pockets or uneven clusters.

Hardware Is Everything

Zippers, snaps, drawstrings, and Velcro closures. A broken zipper on a jacket you paid ¥300+ to ship is devastating. Request close-ups of all hardware. Test zipper smoothness if possible from video QC.

Volumetric weight warning: Puffer jackets take up significant box space. If your shipping line charges volumetric weight (L×W×H÷5000), a bulky jacket can cost significantly more than its actual weight suggests. Request vacuum packaging when available.

Frequently Asked Questions

Lightweight: 600-900g. Midweight puffer: 1-1.5kg. Heavy parka: 2kg+. Always factor volumetric weight — bulky jackets cost more than their actual weight suggests.

Down is warmer and more compressible but more expensive. Synthetic performs better in wet conditions and is usually cheaper. Know what you are buying before QC.
