    Why the Shipping Calculator Matters

Understanding how the calculator works — and its limitations — saves you from the sinking feeling of a ¥300 item becoming a ¥550 total after shipping.

    Actual Weight vs. Volumetric Weight

    The most important concept in the KakoBuy shipping calculator is the distinction between actual weight and volumetric weight. Actual weight is what the package weighs on a scale. Volumetric weight is a calculated value based on package dimensions — it is how much space the package takes up on a plane or truck. International carriers charge based on whichever is higher.

    The formula for volumetric weight: (Length × Width × Height in cm) ÷ 5000 = volumetric weight in kg. So a package that is 40 × 30 × 20 cm has a volumetric weight of (40×30×20) ÷ 5000 = 4.8 kg — even if the actual weight is only 2 kg. You will be charged for 4.8 kg. This is why bulky items like puffer jackets and hoodies can have surprisingly high shipping costs — they are not heavy, but they take up a lot of space.

    Pro TipRequest vacuum packaging for compressible items like puffers, hoodies, and sweaters. Vacuum packing can reduce volumetric weight by 50-70%, saving you significant money on shipping. It costs a small fee (typically ¥5-15) but often pays for itself many times over.

    How to Use the KakoBuy Shipping Calculator

    The calculator is built into the KakoBuy platform — you access it after items arrive at the warehouse. It shows estimated costs for each available shipping line to your destination. Key inputs: destination country, package weight (actual and volumetric), and any additional services (insurance, reinforcement, vacuum packaging). The calculator displays price, estimated delivery time, and any line-specific notes (e.g., "no branded items" for certain lines).

    For pre-purchase estimation — before you commit to buying — use community weight references. A typical pair of sneakers without the box weighs 800-1200g. A hoodie weighs 600-900g. A t-shirt weighs 200-300g. A puffer jacket weighs 700-1000g. Add these up for your planned haul, add 15% for packaging materials, and multiply by the per-kg rate for your preferred shipping line. This gives you a ballpark estimate that is usually within 10-15% of the actual cost.

    How to Minimize Shipping Costs

    Several strategies reduce shipping costs on KakoBuy. First, remove unnecessary packaging — shoeboxes, brand bags, and excess wrapping add volume without adding value. Second, consolidate multiple items into one shipment — per-kg rates drop as total weight increases. Third, use slower shipping lines if you are not in a hurry — the cost difference between FedEx and SAL can be 50% or more. Fourth, always check for active KakoBuy coupons — shipping discount codes are released regularly and can save 5-20%.
