    Not All Spreadsheets Are Created Equal

    Ask ten people on Reddit KakoBuy what the best KakoBuy spreadsheet is and you will get eight different answers. That is because spreadsheet quality is subjective — it depends on what categories you buy, your budget, your tolerance for risk, and how often you want updated links. This guide breaks down the spreadsheet landscape in 2025 and 2026 so you can pick the one that fits your buying style.

    What Makes a Great KakoBuy Spreadsheet

    Before naming specific spreadsheets, let us define the criteria. A great KakoBuy spreadsheet in 2026 has five characteristics. One: active maintenance — updated at least monthly, with dead links removed and new finds added. Two: QC depth — includes QC reference photos or links to community QC posts so you can see what others received. Three: sizing data — weight estimates, size chart links, and fit notes. Four: seller transparency — identifies the seller/store rather than just pasting links. Five: category organization — logically grouped so you can browse, not just search.

    Spreadsheets that score well on all five are rare. Most are strong in 2-3 areas and weak in the rest. The best KakoBuy spreadsheet for you is the one strong in the areas you care about most.

    How We Evaluate Spreadsheets

    Our evaluation process is straightforward. We test links — at least 20 random links per spreadsheet, checking if they resolve to active listings. We verify QC — looking for spreadsheets that show actual QC photos rather than just listing images. We check update dates — spreadsheets older than 3 months without an update are excluded. And we sample the community — reading Reddit discussions and Discord feedback to see which spreadsheets have the best reputation among active buyers.

    The spreadsheet landscape shifts every few months as curators lose interest, sellers decline in quality, or new better alternatives emerge. A spreadsheet that was best-in-class in January 2025 might be abandoned by June 2026. Our recommendations are based on the current state — always check the last-updated date before committing to any spreadsheet.
