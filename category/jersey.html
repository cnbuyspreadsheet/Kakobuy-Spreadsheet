KakoBuy Jerseys: Nail the Details or Do Not Bother

Jerseys are one of the most detail-sensitive categories in any KakoBuy spreadsheet. A jersey with wrong nameplate font, misaligned numbers, or off-color team accents is instantly recognizable to anyone who follows the sport. Our KakoBuy jerseys section tracks sellers with proven accuracy on the details that matter: stitching, heat-press quality, name/number placement, and patch accuracy.

Stitched vs. Heat-Pressed Jerseys

The two main construction types are stitched (twill numbers and nameplates sewn onto the jersey) and heat-pressed (vinyl graphics bonded with heat). Stitched jerseys are more durable and feel more premium, but budget stitched jerseys often have loose threads, uneven letter spacing, or crooked nameplates. Heat-pressed jerseys are lighter and cheaper, but the graphics can peel after repeated washing. Our KakoBuy spreadsheet tags construction type where known.

Pro TipFor basketball jerseys, the armhole trim and neckline shape are the hardest details to get right. Compare your QC photos against retail images focusing on these areas — they reveal factory quality faster than the front number does.

QC Checklist for Jerseys

  • Nameplate font, size, and kerning match retail
  • Numbers are correctly positioned on front and back
  • Team logo/patch is accurately colored and placed
  • Stitching is tight and even — no loose threads
  • Jersey material has correct mesh pattern (if applicable)
  • Size tag and neck tag placement match retail specs
