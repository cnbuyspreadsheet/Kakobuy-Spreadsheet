KakoBuy Headwear: Small Items, Big Details

Headwear on a KakoBuy spreadsheet might seem low-risk — after all, how wrong can a hat go? The answer: very wrong. Embroidery alignment, brim curvature, strap quality, and material thickness all vary significantly between sellers. A cap with a crooked logo is unwearable, and a beanie that is too tight or too loose is a waste of shipping cost. Our KakoBuy headwear section focuses on sellers with consistent embroidery quality and accurate sizing.

Cap Types and What to Check

The spreadsheet covers fitted caps (where size matters — measure your head circumference), snapbacks (more forgiving), strapbacks, dad hats, trucker caps, and beanies. For any cap with an embroidered logo, the QC photo must show the front panel dead-on — even a 2-degree angle can hide crooked embroidery. The KakoBuy Reddit community frequently posts cap QC comparisons; check the threads before ordering from a new seller.

Pro TipHeadwear is the cheapest category to get additional QC photos for. Always request a front-center close-up and a brim-side-angle photo. Total extra cost: about ¥4.

QC Checklist for Headwear

  • Embroidery is centered and level — no tilt or rotation
  • Stitching on the brim is even and tight
  • Strap/clasp functions correctly with clean hardware
  • Inner tags match retail if you care about accuracy
  • Material color matches listing photos
