KakoBuy T-Shirts: What to Look For and What to Avoid

T-shirts are the highest-volume category on any KakoBuy spreadsheet — and the most prone to quality variation. A t-shirt that looks perfect in listing photos can arrive with crooked screen printing, paper-thin fabric, or a neckline that bacon-collars after one wash. Our KakoBuy t-shirts section filters for sellers with consistent batch quality and includes fabric weight notes so you know whether you are getting a premium heavyweight tee or a thin summer layer.

Fabric Weight and Blank Quality

The best KakoBuy t-shirts start with a quality blank. Look for tees in the 220-260 GSM range — these hold their shape, drape well, and do not turn transparent when stretched. Tees under 180 GSM are risky for anything with a graphic print. Sellers who specify the blank manufacturer (e.g., using "vintage washed" or "heavy cotton" in the listing) are generally more reliable than vague listings.

Print Methods: Screen Print vs. DTG vs. Puff Print

Screen-printed graphics on KakoBuy t-shirts are the most durable — they survive dozens of washes without cracking if done correctly. DTG (direct-to-garment) prints are softer but fade faster. Puff print is trendy in 2026 but requires careful washing (cold water, inside out, no dryer). Our spreadsheet tags print type where known.

Pro TipRequest a close-up QC photo of the neck tag. Counterfeit blanks often have misspelled tag text or wrong font — a small detail that reveals a low-tier batch immediately.

QC Checklist for T-Shirts

  • Fabric weight feels substantial — not see-through when held up to light
  • Print is centered and aligned — no crooked graphics
  • Neckline ribbing is tight and elastic — not stretched or wavy
  • Stitching is even on shoulders and side seams
  • Tag text and placement match retail if accuracy matters to you
  • Color saturation matches listing photos
Watch OutBudget t-shirts frequently have misaligned prints — a graphic shifted even 5mm off-center is immediately noticeable when worn. Check print centering carefully in QC photos.
