Why Hoodies and Sweaters Deserve Their Own KakoBuy Spreadsheet Section

Hoodies and sweaters are the backbone of most hauls on KakoBuy. They are versatile, they layer well, and a good-quality hoodie or sweater from the right seller can rival retail pieces at a fraction of the price. But the KakoBuy hoodies and sweaters category has its own pitfalls — fabric weight deception, shrinkage after washing, embroidery alignment, and wildly inconsistent sizing between sellers. This guide walks you through everything our KakoBuy spreadsheet captures to keep your hoodie and sweater purchases on point.

Hoodie Weight: Why GSM Matters More Than You Think

When browsing a KakoBuy hoodies spreadsheet, the single most important spec is fabric weight — measured in GSM (grams per square meter). A hoodie listed at 280 GSM will feel thin and flimsy; 380-420 GSM is midweight and good for daily wear; 500+ GSM is heavyweight territory where the hoodie holds its shape and keeps you warm. Many budget listings claim "heavyweight" but deliver 300 GSM. Smart buyers in the KakoBuy Reddit community always request a weigh-in QC photo for hoodies — the warehouse scale does not lie.

Sweater Materials: Cotton, Wool Blends, and Acrylic

Knit sweaters on KakoBuy come in three broad material tiers. Cotton sweaters (often labeled "cotton knit" or "cotton cardigan") are breathable and easy to care for but can lose shape. Wool-blend sweaters (typically merino or lambswool mixed with nylon or acrylic) offer better warmth-to-weight ratio but require careful washing. Full acrylic sweaters are cheap and pill heavily after a few wears — avoid them unless it is a trend piece you will wear twice. Our spreadsheet tags material composition wherever the seller discloses it.

Pro TipFor hoodies, ask for a close-up QC photo of the embroidery or print. Misaligned chest logos are the #1 flaw on budget hoodies, and they are impossible to fix. Look for clean edges, even stitch density, and no loose threads.

QC Checklist for Hoodies & Sweaters

  • Fabric weight matches the listing claim (request scale photo)
  • Embroidery or print is centered and clean — no misalignment
  • Neck tag and wash tags match retail placement and font
  • Sleeve length and body length match the size chart within 2 cm
  • Drawstrings are the correct material and length (if applicable)
  • Kangaroo pocket stitching is even and secure
  • Ribbed cuffs and hem have good elasticity — not stretched out
  • Color matches the listing; lighting can deceive, so compare with community QC
Watch OutHoodies and sweaters compress significantly during vacuum packaging for international shipping. They will arrive wrinkled. This is normal — a quick steam or wash restores shape. Do not RL a hoodie solely because it looks crushed in QC photos.
